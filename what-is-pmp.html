What is PMP?

PMP stands for Project Management Professional. It is the most recognized project management certification in the world, awarded by the Project Management Institute (PMI) — a non-profit professional organization founded in 1969 and headquartered in the United States.

The PMP credential validates that you have the experience, education, and competency to lead and direct projects across industries. It is recognized in over 200 countries and consistently ranks among the top certifications for salary premium and career advancement.

💡 According to PMI's Earning Power: Project Management Salary Survey, PMP-certified professionals earn on average 33% more than non-certified colleagues.

Who Should Get PMP?

The PMP is designed for experienced project leaders — not beginners. Ideal candidates include:

  • Project managers looking to formalize their experience with a globally recognized credential
  • Team leads or department managers who direct project work
  • Professionals in IT, construction, healthcare, finance, engineering, and other sectors
  • Anyone seeking higher compensation or a move into a senior PM role

If you are just starting out in project management, consider PMI's entry-level CAPM (Certified Associate in Project Management) first, then return to the PMP once you have accumulated sufficient experience.

DomainWeightFocus

Roughly 50% of questions are predictive (waterfall) and 50% are agile or hybrid — a major shift from the pre-2021 exam that was mostly PMBOK-based.

PMP Exam Prerequisites

Before you can apply, you must meet one of the following experience and education requirements:

With a 4-year degree (bachelor's or equivalent)

  • 36 months of project leadership experience within the last 8 years
  • 35 hours of project management education or training

With a high school diploma or associate's degree

  • 60 months of project leadership experience within the last 8 years
  • 35 hours of project management education or training

PMI occasionally audits applications, so keep documentation of your project experience ready. The 35 contact hours can be fulfilled through PMI Registered Education Provider (REP) courses, online training, or university programs.

How Hard is the PMP Exam?

The PMP has a reputation for being challenging. A few things to keep in mind:

  • The first-attempt pass rate is estimated at around 60–70%, though PMI does not publish official figures
  • Most questions are scenario-based — they present a realistic situation and ask what a project manager should do next
  • There is often more than one "good" answer; the exam tests whether you know the best answer in context
  • The agile/hybrid component requires practical understanding, not just vocabulary

Candidates who fail typically do so not because they lack experience, but because they underestimate the need for exam-specific preparation and scenario-based thinking.

How to Study for PMP

A well-structured study plan typically takes 2–3 months of consistent effort. Here's what works:

  1. Study the PMI Examination Content Outline (ECO) — this is the official blueprint of what the exam covers
  2. Read the PMBOK Guide (7th edition) for foundational concepts, and explore the Agile Practice Guide for the agile/hybrid portion
  3. Take a prep course to fulfill the 35 contact hours and structure your learning
  4. Practice scenario-based questions — at least 500–1,000 questions before exam day
  5. Simulate exam conditions — time yourself, take breaks, and get comfortable with the format
  6. Review weak areas using domain-by-domain analysis after each practice session

📝 The most common mistake candidates make is memorizing formulas and processes without understanding when and why to apply them. The PMP rewards judgment, not just knowledge.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does PMP stand for?

PMP stands for Project Management Professional. It is a certification issued by the Project Management Institute (PMI), validating a professional's ability to lead and direct projects.

How many questions are on the PMP exam?

The PMP exam consists of 180 questions to be completed in 230 minutes (3 hours 50 minutes). There are two optional 10-minute breaks. Of the 180 questions, 5 are unscored pretest questions that PMI uses for research purposes — but you won't know which ones they are.

What is the PMP exam pass rate?

PMI does not publish official pass rates. Industry estimates suggest a first-attempt pass rate of 60–70%. The exam does not use a fixed percentage score; instead, candidates receive ratings of Above Target, Target, Below Target, or Needs Improvement for each domain.

How long is PMP certification valid?

PMP certification is valid for 3 years. To maintain it, you must earn 60 Professional Development Units (PDUs) within each 3-year cycle — at least 35 in education and up to 25 in giving back to the profession.

Can I take the PMP exam online?

Yes. Since 2020, PMI offers online proctored exams through Pearson VUE, in addition to in-person testing centers. Online exams require a quiet, private room, a webcam, and a stable internet connection.

Is PMP worth it in 2026?

For experienced project managers, PMP remains one of the highest-ROI professional certifications. PMP holders earn an average of 33% more than non-certified peers (PMI Salary Survey). The credential is increasingly valued as organizations adopt hybrid project delivery methods that the updated PMP exam emphasizes.

PMPとは

PMP（Project Management Professional）は、米国の非営利団体 PMI（Project Management Institute） が認定する、世界で最も権威あるプロジェクトマネジメント資格です。PMIは1969年に設立され、現在は200以上の国・地域でPMPが認知されています。

PMP資格は、プロジェクトをリードする知識・経験・能力を持つことを証明します。IT・建設・医療・金融・製造など、業界を問わず評価される国際資格です。

💡 PMIの調査によると、PMP取得者は未取得者と比べて平均 33%高い給与 を得ています。

PMPを取得すべき人

PMPは実務経験者向けの資格です。以下に当てはまる方に特に適しています。

  • プロジェクトマネージャーとしての実務経験を国際資格で証明したい方
  • チームリードや部門管理職として、プロジェクトを指揮している方
  • IT・建設・医療・金融・エンジニアリングなどの分野で昇進を目指す方
  • 給与アップやシニアPMポジションへの転職を検討している方

プロジェクトマネジメントの経験がまだ浅い方には、PMIのエントリーレベル資格 CAPM（Certified Associate in Project Management） から始めることをおすすめします。

受験資格

PMP受験には以下のいずれかの要件を満たす必要があります。

4年制大学卒業（学士号相当）の場合

  • 過去8年間に36ヶ月以上のプロジェクトリーダー経験
  • 35時間以上のプロジェクトマネジメント教育・研修

高校卒業・短大卒業の場合

  • 過去8年間に60ヶ月以上のプロジェクトリーダー経験
  • 35時間以上のプロジェクトマネジメント教育・研修

PMIは申請内容を抜き打ちで監査することがあります。プロジェクト経験の証明書類を事前に準備しておきましょう。35時間の教育要件は、PMI認定教育機関（REP）のコースや大学の講座で満たすことができます。

PMP試験の難易度

PMPは難関資格として知られています。以下の点を踏まえて準備しましょう。

  • 初回合格率は 60〜70%程度（PMIは公式発表していません）
  • 問題の大半は シナリオ型 — 現実的な状況を提示し、プロジェクトマネージャーとして最善の行動を選ぶ形式
  • 「正しそうな選択肢」が複数ある中で、最も適切な答えを選ぶ判断力が問われる
  • アジャイル・ハイブリッドの領域は、用語の暗記だけでは対応できない実践的理解が必要

不合格になる方の多くは、経験不足ではなく、試験特有のシナリオ型思考への準備不足が原因です。

PMP試験の勉強法

一般的に2〜3ヶ月の継続的な学習が必要です。効果的なアプローチを紹介します。

  1. PMI試験コンテントアウトライン（ECO）を確認する — 試験の公式出題範囲を把握することが最優先
  2. PMBOKガイド第7版を読む — 基礎概念の理解に。アジャイル・ハイブリッド部分は「アジャイル実践ガイド」も参照
  3. 認定教育コースを受講する — 35時間の要件を満たしながら体系的に学べる
  4. シナリオ型練習問題を解く — 試験前に最低500〜1,000問をこなすことが目安
  5. 本番環境をシミュレーションする — 時間を計り、休憩を挟みながら実戦形式で慣れる
  6. 弱点分野を重点的に復習する — 領域別の正答率を把握し、苦手を潰す

📝 よくある失敗は、公式や手順を丸暗記するだけで「なぜそれを使うのか」を理解しないこと。PMPは知識量ではなく、判断力を問う試験です。
